Product Information

What makes your woodfire ovens different from others?

Our woodfire ovens are crafted with premium refractory materials that offer superior heat retention and distribution. Each oven is handcrafted by skilled artisans with decades of experience, resulting in exceptional quality and durability. We also offer customizable features and finishes to match your specific needs and aesthetic preferences.

What are the different models available?

We offer three main models:

  • Classic Woodfire: Traditional design with modern features, ideal for home use.
  • Artisan Pro: Premium model with larger cooking surface and enhanced heat retention, perfect for serious home chefs and small restaurants.
  • Portable Flame: Compact design for versatility and mobility, great for patios, tailgating, and outdoor events.

What materials are your ovens made from?

Our ovens are constructed using a combination of high-quality materials:

  • Premium refractory bricks for the cooking floor
  • High-alumina refractory cement for superior heat resistance
  • Multiple layers of ceramic fiber insulation
  • Stainless steel or powder-coated steel exteriors
  • Custom finishes including brick, stone, or tile exteriors (available on select models)

These materials are carefully selected to maximize heat retention, cooking performance, and durability in outdoor environments.

How long do your ovens last?

With proper care and maintenance, our woodfire ovens are built to last for decades. Many of our customers have been using their ovens for 15+ years with no reduction in performance. We stand behind our craftsmanship with industry-leading warranties:

  • Classic Woodfire: 3-year warranty
  • Artisan Pro: 5-year warranty
  • Portable Flame: 2-year warranty

What type of wood should I use in my oven?

We recommend using seasoned hardwoods for the best cooking results. Oak, maple, hickory, and fruit woods (apple, cherry) are excellent choices. These woods burn hot, clean, and impart subtle flavors to your food.

Avoid using:

  • Softwoods like pine or spruce (they produce too much soot and can impart unpleasant flavors)
  • Treated or painted wood (contains harmful chemicals)
  • Damp or green wood (difficult to burn and produces excessive smoke)

Installation & Setup

Do I need a professional to install my oven?

For our Classic Woodfire and Artisan Pro models, professional installation is recommended due to their weight and the need for a proper foundation. These models typically require concrete slabs and may need to comply with local building codes.

Our Portable Flame model requires no installation and can be placed on any stable, heat-resistant surface. It comes fully assembled and ready to use right out of the box.

We offer installation services in select regions, and can recommend certified installers for customers outside our service areas.

What kind of foundation do I need for my oven?

For permanent installations, we recommend a reinforced concrete slab that extends at least 6 inches beyond the footprint of the oven in all directions. The slab should be at least 4-6 inches thick and properly cured before installation.

Alternatively, our ovens can be installed on existing stone or concrete patios if they meet weight capacity requirements. For specific foundation recommendations based on your model and location, please consult with our installation team.

Do I need a permit to install a woodfire oven?

Permit requirements vary by location. In many municipalities, permanent outdoor structures like woodfire ovens may require building permits, especially if they include foundations, electrical components, or gas connections.

We recommend checking with your local building department before installation. Our customer service team can provide the technical specifications you'll need for permit applications, and our professional installation partners can often assist with permit processes.

Can I install my oven indoors?

Yes, our ovens can be installed indoors with proper ventilation and chimney systems. Indoor installations require specialized design considerations including:

  • A professional-grade ventilation system designed for woodfire cooking
  • Proper clearances from combustible materials
  • Compliance with local building codes and fire regulations
  • Professional installation by certified contractors

Indoor installations typically require additional permits and inspections. Our design team can create custom specifications for indoor installations and recommend qualified installation partners.

How far should my oven be from structures and trees?

We recommend maintaining the following minimum clearances:

  • At least 10 feet from any combustible structure (wooden decks, pergolas, etc.)
  • At least 15 feet from overhead tree branches
  • At least 5 feet from non-combustible structures (stone walls, etc.)

Local building codes may have additional requirements, so always check with your local authorities. Proper chimney height and spark arrestors can also help minimize fire risks.

Usage & Cooking

What can I cook in a woodfire oven besides pizza?

While our ovens excel at making perfect pizzas, they're incredibly versatile cooking tools. Here are just a few things you can cook:

  • Artisan breads and flatbreads
  • Roasted meats and vegetables
  • Baked fish and seafood
  • Casseroles and gratins
  • Desserts like pies, tarts, and cookies
  • Slow-cooked stews and braises

The high heat and unique cooking environment create flavors and textures that simply can't be achieved in conventional ovens.

How long does it take to heat up a woodfire oven?

Heating times vary by model:

  • Classic Woodfire: Approximately 45 minutes to reach optimal cooking temperature (750-850°F).
  • Artisan Pro: About 30 minutes due to enhanced insulation and design.
  • Portable Flame: Just 20 minutes to reach cooking temperature, thanks to its smaller size and efficient design.

These times can vary slightly based on ambient temperature, fuel quality, and other conditions.

How do I know when my oven is at the right temperature?

There are several ways to gauge your oven's temperature:

  1. Infrared thermometer: The most accurate method, pointing a quality infrared thermometer at the cooking surface will give you precise temperature readings.
  2. Visual indicators: When properly heated, the soot on the dome will burn off, leaving it light in color. This typically indicates temperatures above 700°F.
  3. Flour test: Sprinkle a small amount of flour on the cooking surface. At optimal pizza temperatures (750-850°F), the flour should brown in about 10 seconds without burning immediately.
  4. Built-in thermometers: Some of our models feature built-in temperature gauges for easy monitoring.

Can I use my oven in all weather conditions?

Our ovens are built to withstand outdoor conditions and can be used year-round, though there are some considerations:

  • Rain and snow: The ovens themselves are water-resistant, but we recommend using them under some form of shelter (pergola, awning, etc.) for comfort during inclement weather.
  • Extreme cold: In sub-freezing temperatures, you may need longer heating times and more fuel to maintain proper cooking temperatures.
  • Wind: Strong winds can affect flame patterns and temperature consistency. A windbreak or strategic placement can help mitigate these effects.
  • Humidity: High humidity can impact cooking times slightly but won't damage the oven.

For optimal performance in all seasons, consider one of our custom enclosures or shelters designed specifically for our woodfire ovens.

How many pizzas can I cook at once?

The number of pizzas you can cook simultaneously depends on the model:

  • Classic Woodfire 36": 2-3 medium (12") pizzas
  • Artisan Pro 42": 4-5 medium pizzas

For optimal results when cooking multiple pizzas, rotate them during cooking to ensure even browning, and maintain a consistent flame to keep the oven temperature steady.

Maintenance & Care

How do I clean and maintain my woodfire oven?

Regular maintenance is simple:

  1. After each use, allow the oven to cool completely.
  2. Remove ash and debris using a brass brush and metal scoop (avoid steel brushes as they can damage the cooking surface).
  3. For tough spots, use a damp cloth once the oven is cool.
  4. Periodically check the exterior and chimney for any signs of wear or damage.

We recommend a deep clean every 3-6 months depending on usage. Our maintenance kit includes all the tools needed for proper care of your oven.

Do I need to cover my oven when not in use?

Yes, we strongly recommend using a weather-resistant cover when the oven is not in use. While our ovens are built to withstand outdoor conditions, a cover provides additional protection against severe weather, UV exposure, and debris.

Each of our models has a custom-fitted cover available as an accessory. These covers are made from durable, waterproof materials designed to extend the life and maintain the appearance of your oven.

How do I prevent cracking in my woodfire oven?

To prevent cracking in your woodfire oven, follow these best practices:

  1. Proper curing: When first using your oven, follow the curing instructions carefully. This typically involves a series of small fires over several days to gradually dry out any moisture in the materials.
  2. Gradual heating: Always heat your oven gradually. Start with a small fire and slowly build it up over 30-45 minutes.
  3. Protect from moisture: Use a cover when the oven is not in use to prevent water infiltration.
  4. Avoid thermal shock: Never pour water on hot surfaces or rapidly cool the oven.
  5. Maintain the finish: Keep any exterior finishes in good repair to prevent water penetration.

Minor hairline cracks are normal in refractory materials and don't affect performance. If you notice larger cracks, contact our customer service for guidance.

How do I maintain the cooking surface?

The cooking surface (hearth) of your oven requires specific care:

  • Clean with a natural fiber brush to remove food particles and ash after each use
  • For stuck-on food, wait until the oven cools, then lightly scrape with a wooden scraper
  • Never use water or cleaning chemicals on a hot cooking surface
  • If necessary, you can lightly dampen a cloth with water to wipe the surface when the oven is completely cool
  • The natural seasoning that develops over time improves cooking performance, so avoid deep cleaning unless absolutely necessary

Over time, the cooking surface will develop a natural patina that enhances its non-stick properties and contributes to better heat distribution.

What should I do if my oven gets wet inside?

If water gets inside your oven, follow these steps:

  1. Remove any standing water using towels or a shop vacuum.
  2. Allow the oven to air dry naturally for at least 24-48 hours with the door open.
  3. Once the oven appears dry, conduct a gradual curing process:
    • Day 1: Build a very small fire and maintain it for 1-2 hours
    • Day 2: Build a slightly larger fire for 2-3 hours
    • Day 3: Normal fire for 3-4 hours

This gradual approach allows moisture trapped in the refractory materials to escape slowly, preventing steam pressure and potential cracking. Never attempt to rapidly dry out a wet oven with a large fire.
